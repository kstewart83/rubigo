# Low Sensitivity Classification Guide

## Overview

Low sensitivity information is intended for **internal use only**. This data should be available to all employees but is not approved for external distribution without proper authorization.

## Definition

Information classified as **Low** is general business information that supports day-to-day operations. While not highly sensitive, it should remain within the organization to protect competitive advantage and maintain internal cohesion.

## Examples

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Communications | Internal announcements, newsletters |
| Operational | Team meeting notes, project status updates |
| Administrative | Office location details, org charts |
| Reference | Internal policies, procedure guides |

## Handling Requirements

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Low sensitivity information must not be shared externally without explicit manager approval, even if the content seems harmless.

### Access Controls
- Available to all authenticated employees
- No compartment restrictions required
- Standard authentication sufficient

### Permitted Actions
- Share with any internal employee
- Store on internal file shares
- Discuss in internal meetings
- Send via company email

### Prohibited Actions
- Forward to external email addresses
- Upload to public cloud storage
- Discuss with non-employees
- Post on social media

## Declassification

Information may be declassified from Low to Public by:
1. Department head approval
2. Review by Communications team
3. Confirmation that no sensitive elements exist

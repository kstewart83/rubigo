# Moderate Sensitivity Classification Guide

## Overview

Moderate sensitivity information requires **need-to-know access controls**. This data is limited to employees with specific roles, responsibilities, or project involvement. Unauthorized disclosure could cause business harm.

## Definition

Information classified as **Moderate** contains sensitive business details that could impact competitive position, customer relationships, or operational integrity if improperly disclosed.

## Examples

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Financial | Internal budgets, cost analyses, pricing strategies |
| Strategic | Business plans, market research, competitive analysis |
| Personnel | Performance reviews, salary ranges, promotion decisions |
| Legal | Contract negotiations, vendor agreements |
| Technical | System architecture, security configurations |

## Handling Requirements

> [!WARNING]
> Moderate sensitivity information must be protected at rest and in transit. Always verify recipient authorization before sharing.

### Access Controls
- Role-based access required
- Manager approval for new access grants
- Quarterly access reviews recommended
- Audit logging enabled

### Permitted Actions
- Share with authorized individuals only
- Store on secured internal systems
- Discuss in private meetings
- Send via encrypted email when necessary

### Prohibited Actions
- Store on personal devices
- Access from unsecured networks
- Leave visible in public areas
- Discuss in open office spaces

## Labeling Requirements

Documents must include:
- **MODERATE** sensitivity label in header/footer
- Distribution list or audience specification
- Date of classification
- Owner/custodian contact

```
╔════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║  MODERATE SENSITIVITY - INTERNAL USE ONLY          ║
║  Owner: Strategic Planning Department              ║
║  Classified: 2024-01-15                           ║
╚════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

## Incident Response

If moderate information is potentially disclosed:
1. Notify your manager immediately
2. Contact Information Security
3. Document the scope of potential exposure
4. Preserve all related communications
